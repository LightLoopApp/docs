LightLoop is a free, browser-based media management platform designed for churches, schools, and nonprofits who need reliable, unattended screen playback — without IT expertise or monthly fees.

You upload media through the web dashboard, organize it into playlists and feeds, and your Apple TVs download everything locally so screens play without internet, without buffering, and without interruption.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload your media">
    Upload videos, images, audio, and more from the web dashboard. Files are stored securely in your own Google Drive — LightLoop never charges for storage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build playlists and feeds">
    Organize media into playlists (ordered sequences) and assign them to a feed. A feed is what your Apple TV subscribes to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your Apple TV">
    Open the LightLoop app on your Apple TV, enter your Feed ID, and the TV downloads everything automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Play — with or without internet">
    All media is cached locally on the Apple TV. Screens play reliably even if the internet goes down. Changes you make in the dashboard sync in real time when the TV is online.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key concepts

| Term | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Media** | A single file — video, image, audio, livestream, web snapshot, or calendar |
| **Playlist** | An ordered list of media items that plays in sequence |
| **Feed** | A collection of playlists that an Apple TV subscribes to |
| **Feed ID** | The 6-character code your Apple TV uses to connect to a feed |
| **Soundtrack** | A background audio playlist that plays independently behind your main content |
| **Pause Point** | A timestamp where a video automatically pauses until someone advances it |
| **Monitor** | A live check-in roster or calendar rendered as a screen-ready image |
| **Welcome Party** | An animated full-screen greeting triggered when a child checks in |

## What you'll need

- An Apple TV (4th generation or newer) with the LightLoop app installed
