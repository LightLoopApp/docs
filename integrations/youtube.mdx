LightLoop can download YouTube videos and extract audio directly into your media library — no third-party tools needed.

## Importing from YouTube

1. Copy the YouTube video URL from your browser or the YouTube app
2. In the Media library, click **Import → YouTube**
3. Paste the URL
4. Choose the import type:
   - **Download as video** — saves the full video to your Google Drive
   - **Extract audio only** — downloads just the audio track as an M4A file (great for background music soundtracks)
5. Click **Import**

LightLoop fetches the video in the background. It appears in your library when the download completes.

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Media library with a "Downloading from YouTube…" progress indicator on an item card */}

## Best uses

| Use case | Import type |
|----------|-------------|
| Sermon clip or testimony video | Download as video |
| Music video for a worship service | Download as video |
| Background worship music | Extract audio only |
| Podcast or teaching audio | Extract audio only |

## Playlist URLs

You can paste a single video URL. Playlist URLs are not supported — import videos one at a time.

## Copyright considerations

<Warning>
  Only import YouTube content you have the right to use. This includes content your organization created, content licensed for church use (e.g., CCLI Stream+ covers some music), and content under Creative Commons or other permissive licenses.

  Importing copyrighted music or video without a license violates YouTube's terms of service and may constitute copyright infringement.
</Warning>

## File quality

LightLoop downloads the best available quality for the video. For most videos this is 1080p. 4K downloads are supported where available.

Audio-only extracts are saved as high-quality M4A (AAC) files.
