Connect Google Calendar to show upcoming events, service schedules, room bookings, or any calendar data directly on your screens.

## Connecting Google Calendar

<Note>
  Google Calendar integration requires an expanded Google OAuth scope (`calendar.readonly`) beyond what's needed for file storage. You'll be asked to authorize this separately.
</Note>

1. Click your profile picture → **Integrations**
2. Find **Google Calendar** and click **Connect**
3. Sign in with your Google account and grant `calendar.readonly` access
4. LightLoop lists all calendars visible to that Google account

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Google Calendar OAuth screen showing the calendar.readonly permission being requested */}

## Creating a calendar display

1. In the Media library, click **Add Calendar**
2. Select **Google Calendar**
3. Choose which calendar(s) to include (you can show multiple calendars on one display)
4. Configure the display:

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Title** | Label shown at the top of the display (e.g., "This Week at First Church") |
| **Events to show** | How many upcoming events to display (1–20) |
| **Date range** | How far ahead to look (today, this week, 30 days, etc.) |
| **Text size** | Small, medium, or large — adjust for viewing distance |

5. Save — the calendar item appears in your library
6. Add it to any playlist

## Auto-refresh

The calendar display always shows current data. LightLoop re-renders the JPEG image on a regular schedule so upcoming events stay accurate — no manual refresh needed.

## Display layout

Events are displayed in a clean, readable layout optimized for TV viewing:
- Event name (large text)
- Date and time
- Location (if set in the calendar event)
- Color coding by calendar (when showing multiple calendars)

## Use cases

- **Lobby display** — Show this week's services and events as people arrive
- **Hallway** — Show room bookings and group meeting times
- **Kidddo check-in area** — Show when classes start and end
- **Office screen** — Display staff meeting schedule

## Multiple calendars

You can pull from multiple Google Calendars on one display — for example, your "Services" calendar and your "Events" calendar combined. Each calendar can be a different color in the display.

## Disconnecting

1. Click your profile picture → **Integrations**
2. Click **Disconnect** next to Google Calendar

Existing calendar display items will stop updating after disconnection. They'll show the last rendered image until reconnected or deleted.
