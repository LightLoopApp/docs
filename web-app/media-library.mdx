The Media Library is where all your content lives. Every video, image, audio file, web snapshot, and live feed you add to LightLoop appears here.

## Uploading media

### Drag-and-drop upload

Drag video or image files directly onto the Media tab. You can drop multiple files at once.

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Media tab with a file being dragged over it, showing the drop zone highlight */}

### From the Upload button

Click **Upload** to open the file picker. Select one or more files and click **Open**.

Uploads go directly to your Google Drive — LightLoop streams them straight from your browser. A thumbnail is generated automatically on your device before the file finishes uploading so you can see a preview right away.

### Resumable uploads

If your connection drops mid-upload, LightLoop resumes where it left off. Large video files are safe to upload even on spotty connections.

### Importing from Canva or YouTube

Use the import options to bring in content without re-uploading:

- **Canva** — paste a Canva design link; LightLoop fetches it as an image or video ([learn more](/integrations/canva))
- **YouTube** — paste a YouTube URL to download as a video or extract audio only ([learn more](/integrations/youtube))

## Media detail panel

Click any item in the library to open its detail panel on the right side.

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Media detail panel showing a video item with fields for: title, duration, tags, pause points, in/out markers, and "Used in playlists" section */}

### Fields you can edit

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Title** | Display name shown in playlists and on Apple TV |
| **Tags** | Custom labels for filtering and organization |
| **Duration** | Override the display time (for images or short loops) |
| **In / Out markers** | Trim start and end of a video |
| **Pause points** | Timestamps where playback auto-pauses during a service |
| **Subtitles** | Enable or upload a subtitle track |

### Pause points

Pause points let you insert automatic pauses into a video — perfect for curriculum videos with built-in discussion questions.

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Pause points editor showing a video timeline with two pause point markers at timestamps 1:23 and 3:45 */}

1. Open the media detail panel
2. Scroll to **Pause Points**
3. Click **Add Pause Point** and enter a timestamp (e.g., `1:23`)
4. Save

When this item plays on an Apple TV, playback automatically stops at each pause point. Press **Menu** on the Siri Remote to advance to the next section.

### In / Out markers

Trim the beginning or end of a video without re-editing the file:

1. Open the media detail panel
2. Enter an **In** time (when to start) and/or **Out** time (when to stop)
3. Save

<Note>
  In/Out markers don't modify the original file in Google Drive. They're metadata instructions LightLoop sends to the Apple TV.
</Note>

## Organizing with tags

Tags are freeform labels you assign to media items. Use them to filter your library quickly — for example, tag videos by series name, season, or event type.

To add a tag:
1. Open the media detail panel
2. Click the **Tags** field
3. Type a tag name and press Enter
4. Tags are shared across your team (not private)

To filter by tag, use the **Filter** dropdown in the media grid and select one or more tags.

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Tag filter dropdown showing a list of existing tags with checkboxes */}

## Bulk actions

Select multiple items using the checkbox on each card (or click **Select All**), then use the action bar that appears at the bottom to:

- **Delete** selected items (also removes them from any playlists)
- **Add tags** in bulk
- **Remove tags** in bulk

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Media grid with three items checked and the bulk action bar visible at the bottom with Delete and Tag options */}

## Incompatible media

Occasionally an Apple TV will flag a video as incompatible — the file's codec or container isn't supported by that device. These items appear grayed out with a warning badge in the library.

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Media card showing a grayed-out video with a yellow warning triangle badge */}

Incompatibility is reported by the Apple TV itself after it attempts to play the file. To resolve it, re-export the video in H.264 / MP4 format and re-upload.

## Deleting media

To delete a media item:

1. Open the detail panel
2. Scroll to the bottom and click **Delete**
3. Confirm the deletion

<Warning>
  Deleting a media item removes it from all playlists and triggers a re-cache on every Apple TV subscribed to those feeds. It also deletes the file from your Google Drive.
</Warning>
