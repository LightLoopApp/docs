LightLoop has two layers of scheduling: **date-windowed playlists** for seasonal content and **timed broadcasts** for exact time-of-day control.

---

## Date windows (show/hide by date)

Every playlist in a feed can have a start date and end date. Outside those dates the playlist is invisible in the feed — it won't appear on Apple TV.

**When to use:**
- Christmas content that should appear Dec 1 and disappear Jan 1
- A special event playlist for a single weekend
- Summer series content that runs June through August

**How to set it:**

1. Open the **Feeds** tab and select your feed
2. Find the playlist in the feed and click the **schedule icon** next to it
3. Set a **Start date** and/or **End date**
4. Save

<Tip>
  You can add a playlist to a feed now and set a future start date so it's ready to go the moment the date arrives — no last-minute scramble.

## Timed broadcasts (exact time control)

Timed broadcasts let a specific playlist take over a feed at an exact time — automatically, with no one touching anything.

**When to use:**
- Switch from a pre-service loop to a live worship countdown at 9:55 AM every Sunday
- Show a "Service starting soon" screen 10 minutes before each service
- Run a one-time announcement at a specific event

### Creating a timed broadcast

1. Open the **Feeds** tab and select your feed
2. Click **Add Scheduled Broadcast**
3. Configure the schedule:

{/* SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Timed broadcast creation dialog showing: playlist picker, target feed, start time (9:55 AM), duration (30 min), recurrence (weekly), day of week (Sunday) */}

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Playlist** | Which playlist to show |
| **Target feed** | Which feed to broadcast to |
| **Start time** | Time of day (in your account timezone) |
| **Duration** | How long this playlist plays before reverting to the normal feed |
| **Recurrence** | One-time or weekly |
| **Day of week** | (Weekly only) Which day(s) to run |
| **Start date** | When to begin this schedule |
| **End date** | (Optional) When to stop |

### How it works on Apple TV

When a timed broadcast is active:

1. The Apple TV receives a WebSocket push (or detects the broadcast at its next poll)
2. The current playlist smoothly transitions to the scheduled playlist
3. After the broadcast duration expires, the feed reverts to its normal playlist order
4. No one needs to touch the Apple TV or the dashboard

### Viewing and editing broadcasts

All scheduled broadcasts for a feed appear under the **Scheduled** tab within the feed editor. You can edit or delete any broadcast from there.

## Combining both methods

You can use date windows and timed broadcasts together. For example:

- Add your **Christmas playlist** to the feed with a date window of Dec 1 – Jan 1 (it appears automatically in the grid)
- Add a **Christmas service countdown** as a timed broadcast for 10 AM on Dec 24 and Dec 25

The two systems work independently and don't interfere with each other.

---

## Timezone

All scheduled times use the timezone set in your account settings. Make sure your timezone is correct, especially when your server and your building are in different regions.

To check or update your timezone:
1. Click your profile picture → **Settings**
2. Find the **Timezone** field and update it
